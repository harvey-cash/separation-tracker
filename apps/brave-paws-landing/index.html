Core idea

Train below threshold. Track the real pattern. Reduce the guesswork.

Brave Paws centers gradual desensitisation: short planned absences, varied durations, clean data, and practical review over time.

What This Is

Separation anxiety training is a structured behaviour protocol, not a willpower problem.

Dogs with separation anxiety can panic when left alone. The goal is not to "teach them a lesson" or simply wait it out. The work is to create absences that stay short enough for the dog to cope, then extend those absences gradually over time.

Brave Paws follows the gradual-desensitisation approach described in Julie Naismith's Be Right Back: keep sessions below threshold, remove departure cues, vary durations, and let observed behaviour guide the next step.

1

Below threshold

Each absence stays short enough that the dog does not tip into panic.

2

Cue-less departures

Reduce predictable leaving rituals so anticipation does not become the trigger.

3

Randomised durations

Step lengths move up and down so the dog does not learn that each rep always gets harder.

4

Gradual extension

Progress grows across sessions only when the previous work stayed calm and sustainable.

Brave Paws App

Plan, run, rate, and review your training sessions.

The app is a local-first tracker for building timed sessions, running step-by-step countdowns, logging outcomes, and reviewing trends over time. It keeps the training method practical when you are repeating small sessions every day.

  • Create varied session steps with short absences.
  • Run sessions with a guided countdown and total training timer.
  • Record calm, coping, or panicking outcomes with notes.
  • Review history, charts, CSV exports, and inspectable sync.
